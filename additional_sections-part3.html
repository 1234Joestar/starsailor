Parts 1-2 Part 3

Chapter 18

The role of the caregiver: symbolises responsibility and sacrifice. The protagonist's persistence in her duties as a caregiver, despite the loneliness and emotional challenges she faces, reflects her deep concern for her friends and patients.

The closure of Hailsham: symbolises the end of the good old days and the harshness of reality. The closure of Hailsham makes the protagonist feel lost and uneasy, which reflects her nostalgia for her past life and her fear of the uncertainty of the future.

Chapter 19

Boat: symbolises hope and the desire to escape reality. The boat stranded in the swamp becomes a common goal for the protagonist and Ruth and Tommy, symbolising their desire for freedom and a change from the status quo.

The swamp: symbolises hardship and uncertainty. The setting of the swamp reflects the inner confusion of the protagonists.

Chapter 20

Tommy's paintings: symbolising creativity and self-expression. Tommy's paintings are not only a reflection of his personal interests, but also an exploration of his self-identity and the meaning of his existence.

The carer's room: symbolises temporary refuge and solitude. The room described by the protagonist, though humble, is a temporary refuge for her and Tommy, reflecting their inner loneliness and desire for security.

Chapter 21

Madame (Madame): symbolises authority and institutional oppression. The image of Madame reflects society's control and oppression of the clones and its indifference to their fate.

Gallery (Gallery): symbolises the exploration of humanity and the soul. The artwork in the gallery symbolises the inner world and emotional depth of the clones, a testament to them as individuals with souls.

Miss Emily: symbolises the compromise between past ideals and reality. The image of Miss Emily reflects her adherence to the ideals of the past and her helpless compromise with reality.

Morningdale Scandal: symbolises society's fear and rejection of human cloning. The Morningdale scandal reveals society's fear of human cloning, especially the concern that they may replace ordinary humans, reflecting society's complex attitude towards technological progress and ethical issues.

Chapter 22

Gallery: Symbolises the exploration of humanity and the soul. The artwork in the gallery symbolises the inner world and emotional depth of the clones and is a testament to them as individuals with souls.

Miss Emily and Madame: symbolise different attitudes towards the fate of clones. Miss Emily represents sympathy and understanding of the fate of clones, while Madame symbolises indifference and institutionalised control of the fate of clones.

Donation Programme: symbolises the exploitation and use of clones by society. The Donation Programme reveals the purpose for which the clones were created, i.e. to provide organs, and reflects society's exploitation of the clones and disrespect for life.

Disappointment and Hope: symbolises the clones' desire for the future and their disappointment with reality. Although Miss Emily and Madem try to fight for a better life for the clones, in the end they are unable to change the fate of the clones, which reflects the clones' desire for the future and disappointment with reality.

Chapter 23

Field: symbolises freedom and solitude. The vastness and emptiness of the fields reflect the protagonist's inner loneliness and desire for freedom.

Tommy's fourth donation: symbolises the end of life and fear of the future. Tommy's concern about his fourth donation reflects his fear of the end of life and uncertainty about the future.
