Chapter 1-4

1. The Tape: a physical vehicle for Kathy's personal history and emotions; it represents her memories of the past and her longing for loss. (Chapter 1)

2. The Soccer: symbolizes social acceptance and team belonging in this chapter. Tommy's exclusion reflects his isolation among his peers. (Chapter 2)

3. The Mud: The splash of mud on Tommy's body symbolizes his anger and loss of control, as well as suggesting that his social status may be tarnished and devalued. (Chapter 2)

4. Medical Check-ups: The weekly medical check-ups may symbolize the monitoring of the students at school and their status as “products”.

5. Staircase: The chance encounter on the staircase may symbolize the difference in social status between Kathy and Tommy, and the bond that develops between them. (Chapter 3)

6. Art Class: Tommy's behavior in art class may symbolize his desire for self-expression and his creative challenges in school. (Chapter 5)

7. Elephant Painting: Tommy's elephant painting may symbolize his childishness or immaturity and how he seeks approval and attention through art. (Chapter 5)

8. Horses: Ruth's connection with Kathy through the horse game may symbolize the power dynamic between them and how Kathy gains Ruth's approval by accepting the role. (Chapter 6)

9. Woods: The woods, as an element in the setting, may symbolize the unknown, fear, and the mystery that Hailsham students feel about the outside world. (Chapter 6)

10. Pavilion: The gym as a place for students to gather and socialize may symbolize community and a sense of belonging. (Chapter 6)
